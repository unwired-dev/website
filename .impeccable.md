## Design Context

### Users

The site serves two audiences under one brand. The primary audience is a CTO, engineering manager, or startup founder evaluating Unwired for senior frontend implementation, React architecture, design-system work, or targeted advisory. They need to understand Unwired's judgment, credibility, and fit quickly enough to book a frontend consultation. The secondary audience is people interested in privacy-aware, on-device AI tools for personal mail and calendar workflows. Product pages should explain the philosophy clearly and invite them to join the shared product waitlist.

### Brand Personality

Unwired is precise, independent, and quietly bold. It should feel authored by a founder who both thinks carefully and ships production work: technically credible without developer clichés, product-minded without generic startup language, and confident without theatrical self-promotion.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a refined technical-editorial direction: asymmetric, typography-led, crisp, tactile, and restrained. The site should feel like a beautifully produced product journal by someone who builds the work. Avoid gradient-heavy SaaS styling, loud agency-portfolio spectacle, Apple imitation, repetitive card grids, fake product screenshots, and common AI-design tropes.

Dark is the flagship treatment, with a fully intentional light theme. On first visit, respect the visitor's system preference; provide a visible theme control and persist the visitor's explicit choice. Build the palette from deep ink neutrals and warm off-whites. Use signal pink sparingly for small details such as numbers, active states, markers, and precision accents rather than large surfaces.

Pair an expressive grotesk display face with a highly readable humanist sans. Use abstract, code-native product compositions: communication-thread motifs for Unwired Mail and temporal-field motifs for Unwired Calendar. Do not invent product UI. Use one choreographed page entrance, restrained scroll reveals, and precise interaction transitions, all with reduced-motion behavior.

### Design Principles

1. Persuade the consultancy buyer first while giving the product pages a more consumer-facing expression of the same identity.
2. Preserve the Products-first homepage narrative and connect product judgment directly to Frontend Consultancy.
3. Let typography, composition, and rhythm carry the identity; use containers, decoration, and signal pink only when they clarify hierarchy.
4. Keep product storytelling honest: abstract visual systems may suggest Mail and Calendar, but must not imply unbuilt interfaces or unsupported capabilities.
5. Edit copy for clarity and momentum while preserving the canonical terminology and claims in `CONTEXT.md`.
6. Target WCAG 2.2 AA with keyboard access, visible focus, robust contrast in both themes, reduced-motion support, and no color-only meaning.
7. Prefer direct, reliable interactions. The Frontend Consultancy CTA links to the canonical Cal.com booking page instead of opening an embedded modal.
